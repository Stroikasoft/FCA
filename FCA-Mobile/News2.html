In collaboration with the Department of Finance - General Directorate of Customs (GDC), Regional Office for Capacity Building (ROCB) and the World Customs Organization (WCO), the Federal Customs Authority (FCA) holds the Regional Economic Competitiveness Forum (RECF) at Abu Dhabi on 19 and 20 November. The RECF will be attended by experts and officials from the WCO and the World Bank, the Islamic Development Bank (IDB), Organization for Cooperation and Economic Development (OCED), Arab customs, MENA customs, local customs and representatives of chambers of commerce, economic departments and federal local authorities in the UAE. The RECF will be an ample opportunity for all participating officials to share information and experience for two days on economic competitiveness issues in general and customs competitiveness in particular, said Khalid Ali Al Bustani, acting Director General of FCA. The preparatory committee has been keen to reflect the progress made by the UAE in the economic competitiveness in this RECF as the UAE is an important commercial center in the region and the world, he said. Competitiveness, Al Bustani said, has recently occupied a prestigious place in the thought of the leadership and government of the UAE. Thanks to this thought, it has become a strategic goal in the UAE Vision 2021. "After successful application of its standards and requirements, competitiveness has become a landmark of the developmental, economic and social culture of the UAE as it has been established as a solid fact in the real world," said Al Bustani. International reports recognize UAE progress Several international competitiveness institutions have released official report indicating the UAE great progress in all aspects of competitiveness, said Al Bustani. The international reports, including the Competitiveness Yearbook Report 2013, have recognized the increasingly rising competitiveness indicators for the UAE, placing it among he developed countries in terms of governmental efficiency, economic performance, business efficiency, social integration and recruitment, he said. The different governmental, semi-governmental and private institutions in the customs sector play an important role in raising the competitiveness indicators, he indicated. At the federal level, he said, the FCA gives high importance to the competitiveness indicators in the customs business at the top of which the logistic support indicator. Cooperating with the local customs departments, federal authorities and private sector, the FCA seeks to achieve future plans to raise the efficiency of business, support and logistic infrastructure at the state level. The strategies being applied by the local customs departments clearly show how those departments are keen to raise the economic and social competitiveness rates through strategic plans in the economic filed in general and customs field in particular, he said. The local departments aim to build customs capabilities by developing the infrastructure, promoting the institutional culture and applying the future plans with international standards. Global Customs Business Promotion Holding the RECF for the first time in the UAE indicates the UAE keenness to promote the global customs business and push for development and facilitation of international trade in addition to establishing the pillars of the UAE experience in the field of customs and making use of the experiences of different countries in the world in raising the local efficiency in this field, said the acting Director General of FCA. Competitiveness Priority in WCO The competitiveness issue, Al Bustani said, has become a priority in the strategies of the WCO and its affiliated competitiveness office. During the recent WCO meetings, an integrated program on the economic competitiveness has been developed and a decision was made to hold discussion at the level of the WCO regions to discuss indicators and means of the economic competitiveness in the customs field. "Based on this decision the UAE will be the first country in North Africa, Near East and Middle East regions to host the RECF as it has made a bid through the FCA to host it during the WCO recent meetings", he added. The bid is in line with the future strategic plans of the UAE in the field of supporting economic competitiveness, applying the Council of Ministers' Decree No. 285/10/27 of 2013 on moving the UAE to the first place in the global competitiveness indicators, Al Bustani said. "The WCO has several tools to raise the competitiveness rate in the customs business at the member state. These tools include international convention and technical support in building the customs capability of the member states and raising the efficiency of such states in risk managements and other tools. The UAE seeks to utilize these potentials to promote itself on the global competitiveness map, enforcing the instructions of the UAE leadership and government to be number one in the economic competitiveness", he said. Experience Discussion The RECF is significant for the customs leaders as it discusses work papers and developments of the international competitiveness indicators to support the economic growth and competitiveness in the countries across the world by reviewing the best means available in the WCO and the international conventions that support the economic competitiveness in the customs field, Al Bustani said. The RECF aims to utilize the programs available at the WCO in the customs field and achieve the strategic goals of facilitating trade and boosting the international cooperation, he said. It also aims to highlight the international conventions that support the customs sector across the world such as the trade facilitation convention of the World Trade Organization, he added. "The RECF aims to raise the competitiveness level at the customs business in the member states by reviewing the international indicators such as World Bank indicators, promoting the mutual relationships between the customs houses and relevant authorities on border management issues, fostering relations with the private sector for safe and facilitated trade and reviewing the competitiveness rates. Topics of Discussion Sessions The RECF, said Al Bustani, covers many topics through the discussion workshops including an overview of the economic competitiveness, best international practice, the WCO programs and conventions, competitiveness in the UAE government strategy (UAE Vision 2021), the UAE customs experiences and regional projects in the field of economic competitiveness and the regional experiences on "border posts management, transportation and cooperation with private sector). Recognition of Cooperation with Local Departments The acting FCA Director General commanded the local customs departments' keenness to promote and activate the partnership with the FCA to achieve the federal government's strategic goals as mentioned in the UAE Vision 2021. Al Bustani thanked Abu Dhabi GDC at the Department of Finance for supporting organization and sponsorship of the RECF and boosting the cooperation in the joint customs projects. He praised the WCO and the ROCB hosted by Abu Dhabi GDC for being keen to promote opportunities for partnership and experience sharing with the FCA in the field of boosting the UAE customs competitive efficiency.
