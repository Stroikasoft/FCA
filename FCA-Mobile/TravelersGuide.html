Passenger Guide in light of the UAE Customs Laws

The exempt materials authorized to be entered by passengers


  1. Gifts that are not worth more than AED 3000.
  2. 400 cigarettes and 50 cigars maximum. In case of excess quantity, such quantity shall be subject to the prescribed duties.
  3. A maximum of 500 grams of minced tobacco for smoking or pipes or chopped tobacco or molasses tobacco. In case of excess quantity, such quantity shall be subject to the prescribed duties.
  4. Alcoholic beverages of no more than 4 liters, or 2 packages of beer. More quantities shall be confiscated.

The exempt luggage authorized to be entered by passengers:

  1. Optical telescopes.
  2. Film projectors and accessories.
  3. Radios, CD players and discs.
  4. Video and digital cameras and tapes for personal use.
  5. Mobile musical instruments.
  6. One cellular phone, one TV, and one receiver.
  7. Baby carriages.
  8. Personal sports instruments.
  9. Computer and mobile printers.
  10. Disability car and wheelchair.
  11. Calculator.

Terms for exemption from duties:

  • Luggage and gifts should be of personal nature and non-commercial quantities.
  • Passenger shall not be a client of customs, or who is doing business with the material in possession.
  • No cigarettes and alcoholic beverages shall be entered by those under 18.

Source: Common Customs Law of the GCC States and the executive regulations thereof, and the instructions issued by the competent authorities in accordance with law.


Disclosure of cash:
Passengers coming to the State shall disclose the amounts of cash in their possession in excess of an amount of (AED 100.000) one hundred thousand dirham, or the equivalent in other currencies as well as traveler's checks. They shall fill in the disclosure form prepared in accordance with the disclosure law applicable in the State. Those under the age of 18 are not allowed to enter cash amounts over the limit and the cash amounts in their possession shall be added to their accompanying guardians. 

Source: Federal Law No. 4 of 2003 regarding the Criminalization of Money Laundering and the resolutions of the UAE Central Bank.

Smuggling and penalties:


  • Customs smuggling:
Customs smuggling is the admission, transit or exit of the goods without paying the customs duties or in violation of the provisions of restriction and prohibition stated in the laws in force in the State.  
(Article 142 of the Common Customs Law of the GCC States).
  • Forms of smuggling:

  1. A passenger did not proceed with the goods to the first port of entry (customs office).
  2. A passenger did not disclose the incoming or outgoing goods, which have a commercial character to the customs office
  3. When the goods surpass the customs office at entry or exit without being disclosed.
  4. Producing false, fraudulent or fabricated documents or lists or affixing false marks intended to evade the customs duties in whole or in part, or to avoid the provisions of prohibition and restriction.
  5. Transporting or acquisition of prohibited or restricted goods without submitting evidence supporting their legal importation.

(Article 143 of the Common Customs Law of the GCC States).
